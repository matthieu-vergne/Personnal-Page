Dictionary Definitions

- Relevant from Cambridge Dictionaries: - connected with what is happening or being discussed seems rather neutral by establishing a mere relation but, because something unrelated to the matter at hand can be formally related to it with the relationship is unrelated to, everything can be related to anything. This means that we should not speak about a mere relation but something stronger. - correct or suitable for a particular purpose highlights this aspect, establishing that the relation should be supported by the purpose of the matter at hand, thus a goal-driven perspective. - Relevant from Oxford Dictionaries: - closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered. confirm this notion of some relations being more valuable than others because they are closer. A distance for which the dimension should be chosen depending on the need, bringing again a goal-driven perspective. - Relevant from Merriam-Webster: - having significant and demonstrable bearing on the matter at hand highlights again the valuable aspect of the element through its significance, which should be demonstrable rather than merely stated. Two aspects which support again the need to establish a goal-driven perspective from which such a value can be inferred. - affording evidence tending to prove or disprove the matter at issue or under discussion, a particular case where the goal is to prove something, such that a relevant element is an element which can be used in this proof. - having social relevance, a particular case where the goal relates to social relationships, such as people interests or needs. Despite its circularity, this social aspect is an important one when speaking about the expertise of people.

The definitions we saw so far establish a relevant element as an element which shows some positive value in regard to the goal one wants to achieve.
